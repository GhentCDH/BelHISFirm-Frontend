About the data and the Belhisfirm project

Data

The Belhisfirm portal provides access to a selected subset of the SCOB database. SCOB has been documenting Belgian stock exchange history since 1999 and contains hand-collected records of securities listed on the Brussels and Antwerp stock exchanges, including both equity and debt issued by companies and public institutions. The data are compiled from:

  • Official stock exchange lists
  • Historical newspapers
  • Recueil financier (1893 to 1975)
  • Memento der Effecten (1945 to 2011)

Belhisfirm enriches these data with newly digitised material from the Appendices to the Belgian Official Gazette (FR: Moniteur belge, NL: Belgisch Staatsblad, DE: Belgisches Staatsblatt). Since 1873, this publication has been the main source of official information on Belgian companies. It includes:

  • Articles of incorporation and partnership contracts
  • Company names, addresses, and legal changes
  • Appointments and dismissals of directors and auditors
  • Balance sheets and profit and loss accounts (until 1975)
  • Capital structure and securities issuance
  • Dividend and interest payments
  • Corporate reorganisations such as mergers, demergers, and liquidations
  • Information on shareholders and partners

To comply with GDPR, personal data are not published online. Information on individuals in corporate roles (e.g. directors or shareholders) is available through the SCOB database upon formal request.

Data Reuse

This portal is a test version of the Belhisfirm infrastructure. The project is under active development and the current interface does not represent the final version.

Data may be exported as currently available and used at your own risk. Additional datasets and tools will be released and archived through platforms such as Zenodo in later stages of the project.

More Information

BelHisFirm is funded by the FWO Research Foundation Flanders through its large-scale research infrastructure programme (2024–2027). The project is a collaboration between:

University of Antwerp

  • Study Center for Companies and Exchanges (SCOB)
  • Centre for Urban History (CSG)
  • Centre for Social Policy Herman Deleeck (CSB)
  • Institute of Development Policy (IOB)

Ghent University

  • Ghent Centre for Digital Humanities (GhentCDH)
  • Economies, Comparisons, Connections (ECC)

The supervisory team brings together expertise in corporate finance, financial history, business history, inequality and social policy, and development finance. This ensures that the data, technology, and research needs remain aligned throughout the development of the Belhisfirm infrastructure.
